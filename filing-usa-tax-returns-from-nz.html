Filing USA Tax Returns from NZ

2012-04-23

Last updated June 2012.

As i understand it, all USA citizens are required under USA law to report their worldwide income annually on a USA tax return, if they owe USA taxes. See the USA Embassy website for more details. If i'm wrong on this, then please let me know, because it'll save me some time :-)

Here's how i, a non-accountant, prepare my tax return. The procedure has worked for me for the past 4 years with no complaints from the USA Internal Revenue Service. If your finances aren't that complicated, it might work for you too. No promises, though.

15 April

Contact the NZ Internal Revenue Department (http://www.ird.govt.nz/) and ask them to mail you a Summary of Earnings for the past fiscal year. The NZ fiscal tax year is 1 April--31 March. Summary of Earnings letters usually arrive in July.

16 April

Go to the IRS website and see if you need to complete an FBAR form to report your foreign bank accounts. You probably do if you have had a total of USD 10000 in your foreign bank accounts at any point in the previous calendar year. The FBAR is a new form (since 2012?) that carries a hefty fine for incompletion. If you need to fill out an FBAR, then do it and mail it in. In 2012 it was due separate from one's tax return on 30 June.

15 July

Once you receive your Summary of Earnings from NZ IRD, download the the following USA tax forms from the IRS website.

  • f1040: U.S. Individual Income Tax Return
  • f1040sa: Schedule A Itemized Deductions
  • f1116: Foreign Tax Credit
  • f6251: Alternative Minimum Tax—Individuals
  • f4868: Application for Automatic Extension of Time To File U.S. Individual Income Tax Return

16 July

Fill out the forms in this order, though some switching between forms is unavoidable: f1040sa, f1116, f1040, f6251, f4868.

  • For f1040sa, deduct nothing so that your itemized deductions total $0. This odd step allows you to maximize your foreign tax credit in f1116, assuming that you did not earn any money in the USA. If you did earn money in the USA, then depending on how much, you might benefit from not filling out f104sa and taking the standard deduction on f1040 instead. Run the numbers yourself to check which option saves you more money.
  • For f1116, get the NZ dollar-US dollar tax exchange rate for your tax year from http://newzealand.usembassy.gov/irs.html.
  • For f1040, enter 1 April--31 March as your tax year.

17 July

Print, double-check, and sign the forms. Mail them to the IRS. Celebrate.
