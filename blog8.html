Ozymandias

2012-04-24

I met a traveller from an antique land
Who said: Two vast and trunkless legs of stone
Stand in the desert… Near them, on the sand,
Half sunk, a shattered visage lies, whose frown,
And wrinkled lip, and sneer of cold command,
Tell that its sculptor well those passions read
Which yet survive, stamped on these lifeless things,
The hand that mocked them and the heart that fed.
And on the pedestal these words appear:
"My name is Ozymandias, king of kings:
Look on my works, ye Mighty, and despair!"
Nothing beside remains. Round the decay
Of that colossal wreck, boundless and bare
The lone and level sands stretch far away.

~ Percy Bysshe Shelley, 1818

Snow Geese

2012-04-24

Oh, to love what is lovely, and will not last!
What a task
to ask
of anything, or anyone,
yet it is ours,
and not by the century or the year, but by the hours.
One fall day I heard
above me, and above the sting of the wind, a sound
I did not know, and my look shot upward; it was
a flock of snow geese, winging it
faster than the ones we usually see,
and, being the color of snow, catching the sun
so they were, in part at least, golden. I
held my breath
as we do
sometimes
to stop time
when something wonderful
has touched us
as with a match,
which is lit, and bright,
but does not hurt
in the common way,
but delightfully,
as if delight
were the most serious thing
you ever felt.
The geese
flew on,
I have never seen them again.
Maybe I will, someday, somewhere.
Maybe I won’t.
It doesn’t matter.
What matters
is that, when I saw them,
I saw them
as through the veil, secretly, joyfully, clearly.

~ Mary Oliver, 2004

The Last One

2012-04-24

Well they’d made up their minds to be everywhere because why not.

Everywhere was theirs because they thought so.
They with two leaves they whom the birds despise.
In the middle of stones they made up their minds.
They started to cut.

Well they’d made up their minds to be everywhere because why not.

Everywhere was theirs because they thought so.
They with two leaves they whom the birds despise.
In the middle of stones they made up their minds.
They started to cut.
Well they cut everything because why not.
Everything was theirs because they thought so.
It fell into its shadows and they took both away.
Some to have some for burning.

Well cutting everything they came to water.
They came to the end of the day there was one left standing.
They would cut it tomorrow they went away.
The night gathered in the last branches.
The shadow of the night gathered in the shadow on the water.
The night and the shadow put on the same head.
And it said Now.

Well in the morning they cut the last one.
Like the others the last one fell into its shadow.
It fell into its shadow on the water.
They took it away its shadow stayed on the water.

Well they shrugged they started trying to get the shadow away.
They cut right to the ground the shadow stayed whole.
They laid boards on it the shadow came out on top.
They shone lights on it the shadow got blacker and clearer.
They exploded the water the shadow rocked.
They built a huge fire on the roots.
They sent up black smoke between the shadow and the sun.
The new shadow flowed without changing the old one.
They shrugged they went away to get stones.

They came back the shadow was growing.
They started setting up stones it was growing.
They looked the other way it went on growing.
They decided they would make a stone out of it.
They took stones to the water they poured them into the shadow.
They poured them in they poured them in the stones vanished.
The shadow was not filled it went on growing.
That was one day.

The next day was just the same it went on growing.
They did all the same things it was just the same.
They decided to take its water from under it.
They took away water they took it away the water went down.
The shadow stayed where it was before.
It went on growing it grew onto the land.
They started to scrape the shadow with machines.
When it touched the machines it stayed on them.
They started to beat the shadow with sticks.
Where it touched the sticks it stayed on them.
They started to beat the shadow with hands.
Where it touched the hands it stayed on them.
That was another day.

Well the next day started about the same it went on growing.
They pushed lights into the shadow.
Where the shadow got onto them they went out.
They began to stomp on the edge it got their feet.
And when it got their feet they fell down.
It got into eyes the eyes went blind.

The ones that fell down it grew over and they vanished.
The ones that went blind and walked into it vanished.
The ones that could see and stood still
It swallowed their shadows.
Then it swallowed them too and they vanished.
Well the others ran.

The ones that were left went away to live if it would let them.
They went as far as they could.
The lucky ones with their shadows.

~ W. S. Merwin, 1977

Thursday

2012-04-24

Thursday
I have had my dream --like others--
and it has come to nothing, so that
I remain now carelessly
with feet planted on the ground
and look up at the sky --
feeling my clothes about me,
the weight of my body in my shoes,
the rim of my hat, air passing in and out
at my nose --and decide to dream no more.

~ William Carlos Williams, 1921

Filing USA Tax Returns from NZ

2012-04-23

Last updated June 2012.

As i understand it, all USA citizens are required under USA law to report their worldwide income annually on a USA tax return, if they owe USA taxes. See the USA Embassy website for more details. If i'm wrong on this, then please let me know, because it'll save me some time :-)

Here's how i, a non-accountant, prepare my tax return. The procedure has worked for me for the past 4 years with no complaints from the USA Internal Revenue Service. If your finances aren't that complicated, it might work for you too. No promises, though.

15 April

Contact the NZ Internal Revenue Department (http://www.ird.govt.nz/) and ask them to mail you a Summary of Earnings for the past fiscal year. The NZ fiscal tax year is 1 April--31 March. Summary of Earnings letters usually arrive in July.

16 April

Go to the IRS website and see if you need to complete an FBAR form to report your foreign bank accounts. You probably do if you have had a total of USD 10000 in your foreign bank accounts at any point in the previous calendar year. The FBAR is a new form (since 2012?) that carries a hefty fine for incompletion. If you need to fill out an FBAR, then do it and mail it in. In 2012 it was due separate from one's tax return on 30 June.

15 July

Once you receive your Summary of Earnings from NZ IRD, download the the following USA tax forms from the IRS website.

  • f1040: U.S. Individual Income Tax Return
  • f1040sa: Schedule A Itemized Deductions
  • f1116: Foreign Tax Credit
  • f6251: Alternative Minimum Tax—Individuals
  • f4868: Application for Automatic Extension of Time To File U.S. Individual Income Tax Return

16 July

Fill out the forms in this order, though some switching between forms is unavoidable: f1040sa, f1116, f1040, f6251, f4868.

  • For f1040sa, deduct nothing so that your itemized deductions total $0. This odd step allows you to maximize your foreign tax credit in f1116, assuming that you did not earn any money in the USA. If you did earn money in the USA, then depending on how much, you might benefit from not filling out f104sa and taking the standard deduction on f1040 instead. Run the numbers yourself to check which option saves you more money.
  • For f1116, get the NZ dollar-US dollar tax exchange rate for your tax year from http://newzealand.usembassy.gov/irs.html.
  • For f1040, enter 1 April--31 March as your tax year.

17 July

Print, double-check, and sign the forms. Mail them to the IRS. Celebrate.
